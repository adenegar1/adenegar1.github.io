Labor Day

Labor day is the first Monday in September and it honors the working people of America. In order to avoid that very thing, rather than come up with something, here is a poem.

Find Work By: Rhina Espaillat

My mother’s mother, widowed very young
of her first love, and of that love’s first fruit,
moved through her father’s farm, her country tongue
and country heart anaesthetized and mute
with labor. So her kind was taught to do—
“Find work,” she would reply to every grief—
tolled heavy with her passionate belief.
and her one dictum, whether false or true,
Widowed again, with children, in her prime,
she spoke so little it was hard to bear
so much composure, such a truce with time
spent in the lifelong practice of despair.
But I recall her floors, scrubbed white as bone,
her dishes, and how painfully they shone.

Memorial Day

Memorial day is observed on the last Monday of May and it serves to mourn the U.S. military personnel who have died while serving in the United States armed forces.
